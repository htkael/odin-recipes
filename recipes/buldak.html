Buldak: The Fiery Korean Delight

Description

Buldak, which translates to "fire chicken" in Korean, is a beloved dish known for its intense spice and bold flavors. This Korean classic features tender, bite-sized pieces of chicken marinated and cooked in a fiery gochujang (Korean chili paste) sauce. The marinade typically includes soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and sugar, balancing heat with sweetness for a mouthwatering taste. Traditionally, buldak is grilled over an open flame to achieve a smoky char, but it can also be pan-fried or baked for convenience. Served alongside rice cakes, steamed rice, or a cold beer, buldak is a favorite among spice enthusiasts. Whether enjoyed as a main dish or a shared appetizer, this fiery delicacy promises a flavor-packed experience that keeps diners coming back for more. Perfect for anyone who loves bold and adventurous flavors!

Ingredients

  • Udon noodles
  • Soy sauce
  • Gochuchang
  • Gochugaru
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Egg

Steps

  1. Fill pot with water and boil
  2. Put noodles and egg into boiling water. Let boil for five minutes
  3. Strain noodles, preserving 50mL of water
  4. Put noodles into bowl with water
  5. Add seasonings and mix
  6. Enjoy!
